At Scootz, we offer a comprehensive selection of electric mobility scooters designed to suit a variety of needs. Whether you're looking for portability, versatility, or heavy-duty options, we’re here to help you find the perfect fit. *Not sure where to start?* Here’s a quick guide to the different types of mobility scooters we provide:

Portable/Travel Scooters

As the name suggests, portable or travel scooters are designed for life on the go. These models disassemble easily, making them simple to transport in the trunk of most cars. With some models featuring a heaviest piece as light as 22 pounds, they’re easy to handle. Plus, sealed batteries make them safe for air travel. Compact and lightweight, these scooters are best suited for users under 5'10" and 300 pounds. Popular options include models from Invacare and Pride.

3-Wheel Scooters

Offering a perfect balance of comfort and maneuverability, 3-wheel scooters are a great choice for both indoor and outdoor use. These mid-sized scooters provide enhanced features, such as power-elevating seats, and can support up to 350 pounds. Whether you’re navigating tight indoor spaces or exploring outside, this versatile category has something for nearly everyone. Top brands include Golden and Pride scooters.

4-Wheel Scooters

If stability and outdoor performance are your priorities, 4-wheel scooters might be the ideal option. These models are designed for rugged terrains, with higher ground clearance, larger tires, and speeds of up to 10 mph. While their wider turning radius makes them less suited for indoor use, they excel in outdoor environments, making them perfect for both work and leisure.

Heavy-Duty Scooters

For individuals weighing over 350 pounds, heavy-duty scooters provide robust support and durability. With both 3-wheel and 4-wheel options available and weight capacities of up to 500 pounds, these scooters are built to meet a range of needs while ensuring comfort and reliability.

No matter your requirements, Scootz has the right mobility scooter to help you move with ease and confidence.

Can I take my scooter on an airplane?

All mobility scooters sold by Scootz are equipped with "maintenance-free" batteries, which are airline-approved. Whether your battery type is "gel cell" or "sealed lead acid," we recommend contacting your airline directly to confirm their specific policies and secure approval prior to travel.

Can I take my scooter on a cruise?

Many cruise lines have recently updated their policies regarding mobility scooters. Most now require that scooters fit through stateroom doors (typically 22 inches wide), can be folded or disassembled for storage, and are not left in hallways. Once inside your cabin, scooters should be stored under the bed or in a closet. Since each cruise line has its own set of regulations, it’s best to check their corporate website for the most up-to-date requirements before your trip.

Is shipping free?

Yes, Scootz offers free standard ground shipping on all electric scooters.

If your scooter doesn’t meet your expectations, you can return it within 30 days, provided it is unused (tires must be clean, and the scooter free from scratches) and in its original packaging. Portable/Travel Scooters

As the name suggests, portable or travel scooters are designed for life on the go. These models can be easily disassembled to fit into the trunk of most cars, with some options featuring a heaviest piece weighing just 22 pounds for effortless lifting. Their sealed batteries make them airline-approved (*lithium batteries must be removed and carried on board). Compact by design, they’re best suited for users under 5'10" in height and weighing less than 300 pounds. Top brands in this category include Drive Medical, Golden Technologies, and Pride Mobility.

Full-Size Scooters

Full-size scooters are ideal for users seeking more power, comfort, and advanced features. While some models can be disassembled, the individual pieces are typically bulkier and much heavier than those of travel scooters, making frequent disassembly impractical. These scooters come with a variety of standard features and optional upgrades, delivering excellent performance on smooth outdoor surfaces and offering extended ride times. However, their larger turning radius and wider dimensions mean they may be less maneuverable indoors. Before purchasing, measure your doorways and consider any tight spaces, like narrow hallways or bathroom turns, that may be challenging to navigate.

Recreational Scooters

Recreational scooters are built for speed and long distances, with ranges of up to 43 miles and top speeds of 15 mph. Unlike traditional mobility scooters, some recreational models lack medical features, such as backrests, and may require a hand brake for control due to their higher speeds. These scooters are fun, zippy, and perfect for leisurely outings, neighborhood commutes, or even a trip to the golf course.

Heavy-Duty Scooters

For individuals weighing over 400 pounds, heavy-duty scooters provide a reliable and comfortable mobility solution. Available in both 3- and 4-wheeled designs, as well as full-size and travel-friendly models, these scooters offer weight capacities of up to 500 pounds. They feature wider seats and enhanced suspension systems for a smoother ride. With a variety of options to choose from, there’s a heavy-duty scooter designed to meet your specific needs.

Fully Assembled Weight: Scooters vary significantly in weight once assembled, ranging from 50 to 200 pounds. Consider the weight based on your specific needs and usage.

Turning Radius: The turning radius refers to the smallest circular turn a scooter can make. A smaller radius ensures easier maneuverability, especially around tight corners, enhancing overall ease of use.

Ground Clearance: This is the distance between the ground and the scooter’s undercarriage. A lower clearance provides greater stability, while higher clearance is essential for outdoor use to avoid damage from uneven terrain or obstacles.

Maximum Recommended Incline: This specifies the steepest incline the scooter can safely handle. It’s a critical factor to consider for outdoor use, particularly on slopes like sidewalks, ramps, or hilly areas.

Portability: Transportation options for your scooter are essential to think about. Travel scooters are designed for portability and can be disassembled to fit into a car trunk. In contrast, full-size scooters typically require a vehicle lift or ramp for transport.

Headlights: Built-in headlights are perfect for navigating dimly lit spaces or dark hallways, providing better visibility and safety.

Suspension: Scooters equipped with suspension systems offer a smoother ride by absorbing bumps and cracks on sidewalks, making outdoor travel more comfortable.

Seat Options: Scooters are available with two main types of seats: stadium-style seats with lower backrests and full-back captain seats. Both seat styles come in various widths and depths, so it’s important to choose a seat size that delivers optimal comfort and support for your needs.
