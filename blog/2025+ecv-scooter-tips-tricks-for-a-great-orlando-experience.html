Why Rent an ECV in Orlando?

With so much to see and do in Orlando, having an ECV can be a game-changer. For those with mobility challenges or anyone looking to reduce fatigue, an ECV ensures you can enjoy more of what Orlando offers without compromising comfort or energy.

  • Explore More: Large attractions like Disney World, Universal Studios, and the Orlando Convention Center can cover significant distances, making an ECV essential for full exploration.
  • Reduce Fatigue: Make your day stress-free by eliminating the need for excessive walking, allowing you to focus on having fun.
  • Convenience Meets Independence: Renting an ECV offers an ideal mix of freedom and flexibility, giving you control over your pace and schedule.
     

Tips for Using an ECV in Crowded Orlando Spaces

1. Master the Controls Before You Go

Before entering a busy theme park or shopping area, take a few minutes to familiarize yourself with your ECV’s controls. Practice accelerating, braking, turning, and reversing to ensure you’re confident and comfortable in its operation.

2. Plan Your Route Ahead of Time

Theme parks and attractions in Orlando can get crowded, making it wise to plan your route beforehand. Familiarize yourself with maps that show accessible paths, elevators, and ramps. Many attractions offer guides specifically designed to help ECV users get around comfortably.

3. Stay in Designated Paths

Most venues, including parks and malls, have designated areas for ECVs. These paths are wide enough to accommodate scooters and other mobility aids, ensuring safe and efficient travel for everyone.

4. Be Mindful of Crowds

When navigating through a crowd, maintain a slower speed and use your horn or bell (if available) to alert others to your presence. Polite communication goes a long way in ensuring smooth navigation.

5. Secure Priority Seating Lines

Many attractions in Orlando provide accessible or priority queueing options for ECV users. Be sure to ask staff members about accommodations to avoid waiting in long lines.

6. Park Responsibly

If you’re visiting an attraction or restaurant and don’t need your ECV during specific activities, park it in designated areas to leave pathways clear for others.
 

Tips for Maximizing ECV Battery Life

Running out of battery in the middle of a fun-filled day is the last thing you want. These tips will help you extend battery life and avoid interruptions.

1. Charge Fully Overnight

Start your day with a fully-charged battery. ECVs typically require several hours to reach full charge, so plug it in overnight.

2. Avoid Overloading Your ECV

Carrying unnecessary weight can drain your ECV’s battery faster. Pack only what you need and distribute the load evenly to ensure optimal performance.

3. Turn Off When Not in Use

If you plan to stay stationary for extended periods, such as during meals or watching shows, switch off your ECV to conserve power.

4. Monitor Battery Levels

Keep an eye on your ECV’s battery indicator throughout the day. If your charge is running low, head to a charging station (available at many major parks and venues in Orlando) before the battery dies entirely.

5. Choose Your Terrain Wisely

Navigating steep inclines and rough surfaces can consume more energy than flat, even paths. Stick to smoother terrain whenever possible to extend battery life.
