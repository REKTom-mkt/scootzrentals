Where to Leave Your Scooter at Walt Disney World

 

Planning a trip to Walt Disney World is exciting, but it also involves a lot of logistics, especially if you or a loved one relies on a mobility scooter. One of the most common questions we get is, "Where can I leave my scooter?" Whether you're catching a show, sitting down for a meal, or heading back to your hotel for the night, knowing the rules and best practices for scooter parking will make your visit much smoother.

Navigating the parks with a mobility aid should be a stress-free experience. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know about parking your scooter at shows, restaurants, and resorts across the Walt Disney World property. By the end, you'll feel confident managing your scooter, allowing you to focus on making magical memories with your family.

General Scooter Parking at Walt Disney World

Walt Disney World is designed to be accessible, and that includes providing designated parking areas for Electric Conveyance Vehicles (ECVs), also known as mobility scooters. You'll find these designated spots near the entrances of most attractions, theaters, and restaurants.

These areas are typically marked with signage, and while they aren't always covered, they are conveniently located. It's a good practice to always look for these official parking zones. Leaving your scooter in an undesignated area can obstruct pathways and may result in a Cast Member moving it to a safer location. If you return to find your scooter isn't where you left it, don't panic. Check nearby designated parking areas first, then ask a Cast Member for assistance.

A helpful tip is to make your scooter easily identifiable. Tying a brightly colored ribbon or attaching a unique tag can help you spot it quickly in a sea of similar models. This is especially useful if you're using a popular service for Walt Disney World scooter rental in Orlando, FL.

Leaving Your Scooter at Shows and Attractions

Most attractions at Walt Disney World require you to transfer from your scooter to the ride vehicle. For those that do, you'll find convenient scooter parking right near the attraction's entrance or at the point where the queue becomes inaccessible for scooters.

Attractions with Transfer Requirements

For rides like "Peter Pan's Flight," "Haunted Mansion," or "Test Track," you will need to park your scooter before entering the main queue. Cast Members are always on hand to direct you to the nearest ECV parking. After the ride, you simply return to the same spot to retrieve your scooter.

Attractions You Can Ride With Your Scooter

Some attractions are designed to be fully accessible, allowing you to remain in your scooter or wheelchair for the entire experience. These include:

  • "Living with the Land" at EPCOT
  • "The Seas with Nemo & Friends" at EPCOT
  • Kilimanjaro Safaris at Animal Kingdom
  • "Mickey & Minnie's Runaway Railway" at Hollywood Studios (accessible ride vehicle available)
  • "it's a small world" at Magic Kingdom (accessible boat available)

For these attractions, Cast Members will guide you through a separate, accessible queue line.

Parking at Live Shows

Theaters and shows like the "Festival of the Lion King" or "For the First Time in Forever: A Frozen Sing-Along Celebration" have designated areas where you can park your scooter. You'll typically leave your scooter in a marked area at the back or side of the theater and then walk to your seat. If you are unable to transfer to a standard seat, these venues also offer integrated wheelchair and companion seating spaces. It's always a good idea to arrive a little early to speak with a Cast Member about the best place to park and sit.

Parking Your Scooter at Restaurants

Dining is a huge part of the Walt Disney World experience, and restaurants are well-equipped to handle guests with mobility scooters.

Table Service Restaurants

When you check in for your reservation at a table service restaurant, let the host know you have a scooter. They will typically direct you to a nearby parking area, often just outside the restaurant's entrance or in a corner of the lobby. You can then walk to your table. If you need to remain in your scooter, inform the host. They can usually accommodate this by removing a chair from a table to create space, though this may not be possible at all locations, especially those with fixed booth seating.

Quick Service Restaurants

Quick service locations are generally more spacious. You can often navigate the ordering area with your scooter and take it with you to an open table. Look for tables with movable chairs that provide enough room to park your scooter beside you without blocking walkways. Many outdoor seating areas are particularly convenient for scooter users. Be mindful of keeping pathways clear for other guests.

Overnight Scooter Storage

What do you do with your scooter when the park day is over? The answer depends on where you are staying.

Staying at a Disney Resort Hotel

If you are a guest at a Walt Disney World Resort hotel, you can bring your scooter back to your room with you. Hotel rooms are spacious enough to accommodate a scooter, and you can charge it overnight so it's ready for another full day of adventure. Simply drive it into your room and plug it into a standard wall outlet. It's best to park it in a corner or an out-of-the-way spot to keep your room tidy.

Some guests prefer to leave their scooter just outside their room door in the hallway. While this is sometimes tolerated, it's not recommended as it can obstruct the corridor. The safest and most secure option is to always bring it inside your room.

Staying Off-Property

If you're staying at a hotel outside of Walt Disney World, you'll need to confirm their policy on mobility scooters. Most hotels are accommodating and will allow you to bring the scooter to your room for overnight charging. It's wise to call ahead and confirm this before your trip.

If you are using a Walt Disney World scooter rental in Orlando, FL, some companies may offer to pick up the scooter from your hotel and redeliver it fully charged the next morning. Check with your rental provider to see what services they offer.

A Final Word on Scooter Etiquette

Using a scooter at Walt Disney World comes with a responsibility to be considerate of others. Here are a few final tips:

  • Watch Your Speed: The parks are crowded. Operate your scooter at a walking pace to ensure the safety of yourself and those around you.
  • Use Your Horn Sparingly: A gentle beep can be helpful, but overuse can be disruptive.
  • Be Patient: Navigating crowds takes time. A little patience goes a long way in ensuring a pleasant experience for everyone.

By keeping these guidelines in mind, you can navigate the parks with confidence and ease.
