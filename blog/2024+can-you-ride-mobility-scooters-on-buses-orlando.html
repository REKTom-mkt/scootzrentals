Can You Ride Mobility Scooters on Buses in Orlando?


If you're visiting Orlando and rely on a mobility scooter to get around, you may be wondering if you can use public transportation, specifically buses, with your mobility device. Fortunately, Orlando offers a range of accessible travel options to accommodate individuals who use mobility scooters. Whether it's public buses or private services, this guide will walk you through everything you need to know about riding mobility scooters on buses in Orlando and how to make your trip convenient and stress-free.

Are Mobility Scooters Allowed on Orlando Buses?

Yes, mobility scooters are allowed on buses operated by the Lynx public transportation system in Orlando. Lynx buses are designed to be accessible to people with a wide range of mobility needs, including passengers who use wheelchairs or mobility scooters.

Most buses are equipped with:

  • Ramps or Lifts: These allow mobility scooters to easily board and disembark.
  • Designated Wheelchair/Scooter Spaces: These areas are secured with straps or clamps to ensure your scooter stays safe during transit.
  • Ramp Operators: Drivers are trained to assist passengers with boarding.

If you plan to use your mobility scooter on a Lynx bus, make sure your scooter meets the size and weight requirements set by Lynx. Mobility scooters must generally fit within the footprint of a standard wheelchair, which is approximately 30 inches wide and 48 inches long when measured fully extended. Weight limits, when combined with the rider, are typically around 600 pounds.

How to Check Accessibility Before Boarding

To ensure a hassle-free experience, follow these steps before planning your ride with a mobility scooter:

1. Check Compatibility

Call Lynx customer service or visit their website to confirm your scooter meets their size and weight restrictions. Some smaller or lightweight scooters may be better suited for bus rides.

2. Plan Your Route

Identify which bus lines you'll be taking and whether they serve accessible routes. Lynx's website and mobile app include accessibility features that highlight accessible buses and stops.

3. Board During Off-Peak Hours

If you’re nervous about overcrowding, plan bus rides earlier in the day or mid-afternoon to avoid high traffic times.

4. Practice Boarding

If you’re unfamiliar with using your mobility scooter on public transportation, practice using ramps and lifts before your trip. Many scooter rental providers will provide guidance on using their vehicles.

5. Coordinate with the Bus Driver

When boarding, notify the bus driver that you’re using a mobility scooter. They’ll assist with deploying ramps or lifts and securing your scooter in the designated space.

Alternative Accessible Travel Options

Although Lynx buses are mobility-scooter-friendly, Orlando offers additional transportation options that may better suit your travel plans.

1. SunRail

SunRail, a commuter train in Orlando, is also scooter-accessible. Similar to Lynx buses, SunRail services feature lifts and designated spaces for mobility devices.

2. Private Rideshare Services

Services like Lyft and Uber now offer Uber WAV (Wheelchair Accessible Vehicles) in select areas of Orlando. These vehicles are designed to accommodate mobility scooters and wheelchairs, making them a great alternative if you prefer private transit.

3. Theme Park Transportation

Major Orlando attractions like Walt Disney World and Universal Studios also provide accessible buses and shuttles. If you're planning visits to theme parks, be sure to check their specific rules about boarding with a mobility scooter. Many services on these properties are free and specially equipped for passengers with disabilities.

4. Special Assistance Vans

For personalized transportation, companies such as Access Lynx provide door-to-door ride options. These services are great for those who require extra assistance or have particular travel needs. Make reservations ahead of time to ensure availability.

Important Tips for Maximizing Mobility in Orlando

Keeping these tips in mind can ensure a smooth and enjoyable experience while using your mobility scooter to travel throughout Orlando:

  • Charge Your Scooter Fully

Before heading out for the day, make sure your scooter’s battery is fully charged. Nothing’s worse than running out of power while waiting for public transit.

  • Store Contact Numbers

Save phone numbers for Lynx’s customer support, Scootz Mobility, and other relevant transport services in case you need assistance.

  • Respect Bus Policies

Be mindful of Lynx’s guidelines regarding scooter securement, boarding, and disembarking. Cooperation with transit staff makes everyone’s experience more pleasant.

  • Prepare for Weather

Orlando is known for its unpredictable weather. Equip your scooter with a waterproof cover and be ready for rain, especially during Florida's summer months.

With proper preparation and a well-suited scooter, public transportation in Orlando is a convenient, accessible option.
